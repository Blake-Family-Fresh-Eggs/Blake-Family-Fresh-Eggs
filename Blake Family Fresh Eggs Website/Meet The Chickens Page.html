MEET THE CHICKENS:

1. Baba Yaga (Mixed Breed, Medium Light Brown Eggs)
This hen is not liked by any other chicken. She is an outcast, but for a reason. Baba Yaga picks on any chicken younger than her. She will get a beak full of feathers from a young chicken any chance she gets.

 

 

2. Becky White (Leghorn, Medium White Eggs)
Becky White is an extremely good egg-layer. She consistantly lays a good egg every day. She is not that friendly with the other chickens, but she doesn't purposely go after the younger ones like Baba Yaga does. Becky doesn't like to be picked up, and will run from you if you try to do so. However, she, like all the other chickens, will come running if you have a some corn in your hand.

 

 

3. Snap (Partridge Littau Brahma, Large Speckled Brown Eggs)
Snap is the biggest bird we have. She won't run when you go to pick her up, but she isn't exactly happy with it either. Snap is also a good egg layer.

 

 

4. Crackle (Partridge Littau Brahma, Large Speckled Brown Eggs)
Crackle is the talker of the flock. She is also near the top of the pecking order. Crackle will always make her voice heard. If she wants something, she will voice it loudly. She is well liked by most of the chickens, and is a good layer.

 

 

5. Pop (Partridge Littau Brahma, Large Speckled Brown Eggs)
Pop despises being picked up or even touched. Pop will sprint away if you even start to reach out your hands to pick her up. However, Pop is not a mean chicken, as she is well-liked by the other chickens. In fact, Pop was the first to accept the new chicks when they were first introduced.

 

 

6. Chicken (Ameraucana, Large Green/Blue Eggs)
Chicken is the friendliest of all the chickens. She will follow you around, and fly up onto your lap if you sit down. Chicken is liked by all except Baba Yaga, but no chicken likes Baba Yaga either. Chicken is a good egg layer too, like the rest of the Ameraucanas.

 

 

7. Nugget (Ameraucana, Large Green/Blue Eggs)
Nugget is a chunky chicken, for an Ameraucana that is. She is the biggest Ameraucana, but isn't even close to Littau Brahma Snap's size. Nugget is friendly, with both humans and chickens alike.

 

8. Cheesecake (Ameraucana, Large Green/Blue Eggs)
Cheesecake was the first Ameraucana to start laying. She started laying a full week before any of the other Ameraucanas did. Cheesecake is also a copycat, or, in this case, a copychicken. She copies the noise Littau Brahma Crackle makes.

 

9. Sweet (Ameraucana, Large Green/Blue Eggs)
Sweet is the funniest of the Ameraucanas. She is a talker, like Littau Brahma Crackle. Sweet is at the top of the pecking order when it comes to the Ameraucanas. Sweet is also really friendly with people and other chickens. Sweet really fits her name.

 

10. Sour (Ameraucana, Large Green/Blue Eggs)
Like the chicken above, Sour really fits her name. Sour will run away from you if you get near her. However, Sour has some redeeming qualities. If you sit down, Sour will jump on your lap. Sour is actually friendly, she just despises being picked up.

 

11. Gray (Ameraucana, Large Green/Blue Eggs)
Gray is completely grey in color, but her name is spelled the British way. Gray is sneaky, and will steal food right out from under the other chickens beaks. Gray is friendly, and will come running if you have food in your hand.

 

12. Pot Pie (Ameraucana, Large Green/Blue Eggs)
Pot Pie is the most troublesome out of all the Ameraucanas. She will always social distance herself from you. She hates being picked up, or even touched for that matter. She will always sprint off if you even start to move in her direction. However, she cannot resist the temptation of corn, and will come running if you have some.

 

13. Rue (Ameraucana, Large Green/Blue Eggs)
Rue was named after the little girl in the Hunger Games. Rue is pretty high up on the friendliness factor. She will jump right up onto your lap if you sit down. Rue also has a taste in footwear, literally. She will peck at your shoes any chance she gets.

 

NOTE: More chickens will be added to this list in the future.
